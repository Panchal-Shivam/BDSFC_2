What is CIBIL?

CIBIL, or Credit Information Bureau (India) Ltd, is the authorized agency that provides credit records and scores to banks and money lenders to help them assess if a borrower would be able to repay the loan. All your credit information is provided to CIBIL on a monthly basis. This includes information on your EMI and credit card payments (including any late or missed payments), balance outstanding, any change in your total credit limit, and all other details relevant to your credit profile.

Understanding Your CIBIL Score

CIBIL summarizes all your credit history in the form of a three-digit number, which ranges between 300-900. A high score indicates you are a less risky borrower, increasing your chances of availing a loan, while a low score decreases your probability of getting a loan.

Loan Worthiness Based on CIBIL Score

  • 300-599: A score below 600 is considered very low. Chances of getting a loan with such a low score are slim. Improve your credit score before applying to avoid bank rejection, which would further lower the score.
  • 600-749: This score range is not ideal, but some lenders may approve your loan based on factors like employment, salary, and existing loans.
  • 750 and above: A score over 750 is excellent, making loan approval easier. With a high score, you can negotiate better rates, as lenders value creditworthy borrowers.

If Your CIBIL Score is Below 700

Here are some steps to consider:

  • Avoid public bank inquiries if your score is below 700, as inquiries impact your score. Public banks are less likely to approve loans for scores under 700, but certain NBFCs may offer loans with scores as low as 650 if you have a valid reason.
  • Always check your CIBIL score before applying to avoid multiple inquiries affecting it. Credenc provides a single-window platform to compare and choose lenders without physical visits, ensuring quick approval and the best interest rates.
